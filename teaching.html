Teaching

Teaching is a core part of my job. As an educator, I am dedicated to inspiring curiosity, fostering critical thinking, and empowering students to become rigorous researchers. With a focus on creating engaging and interactive learning experiences, I strive to teach not only knowledge but also essential skills in conducting psycholinguistic research. Through continuous reflection and innovation, I am committed to adapting my teaching methods to meet the diverse needs of every learner.

Research Methods Training

  • Designed and delivered lecture-style training on experimental design and statistics, e.g., Behavioural Experiment: Paradigms and Design; Eye-tracking: Principles and Design; EEG: Principles and Design; Introduction to Statistics for undergraduate students.
  • Designed and delivered practical-style training on software and coding, e.g., Acoustic Analysis with Praat; Online Experiments with Gorilla and PsyToolkit; practicals on E-Prime, Experiment Builder, and PsychoPy; participant recruitment with Prolific and SONA; EEG/ERP data pre-processing; EEG/ERP data analysis; forced alignment.
  • Designed and delivered small-group hands-on training on EEG and eye-tracking experiment operation.

Supervision

Cambridge Small-Group Teaching

  • Taught in small groups for Li3: Language, Brain, and Machine, formerly Language, Brain, and Society, covering topics in sociolinguistics, language acquisition, psycholinguistics, and computational linguistics.
  • Delivered high-quality Chinese language teaching for the Modern Chinese course, including preparing tailored materials.

Supervision on Research Projects

  • Supervised projects on experimental phonetics and psycholinguistics.
  • Yilin Wang: Tonal Production and Tone Sandhi in Southern Min-accented Mandarin.
  • Yujie Du: Deficits in Visuo-spatial Working Memory in Dyslexic Population? A Systemic Review and Meta-analysis.
  • Yuge Liu: Influence of Sentential Intonation on Tone Production in Nanjing Mandarin.
  • Weiling Du: Code-switching in Bi-dialectal Speakers of Cantonese and Mandarin.
  • Joyce Li: A Meta-analysis on Fear Conditioning in Autistic Population.

Lectures

  • Delivered lectures on psycholinguistics, introducing theories and methodologies with a focus on lexical processing.
  • Helped students develop an understanding of the mechanisms of lexical processing and skills in psycholinguistic experimentation.
  • Supervised students in completing research proposals appropriate for applications or theses.
  • Delivered general training lectures on topics such as research proposal writing and citation. These lectures were live-streamed and recorded, with live audiences of more than 300 students for each session, and each recording has been watched by more than 1,000 people.
