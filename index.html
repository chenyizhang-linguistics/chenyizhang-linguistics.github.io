About Me

I am a fourth-year PhD student in Computer Science Theory at Stanford University, advised by Adam Bouland and Aaron Sidford.

I received my undergraduate degree from the Yao Class at Tsinghua University, where I studied theoretical computer science and mathematics.

Research Description

My research focuses on theoretical aspects of quantum computing and computational complexity. I am interested in understanding the power and limitations of quantum algorithms, as well as developing new algorithmic techniques for optimization and other computational problems.

Feel free to reach out if you are interested in discussing research or potential collaborations.
